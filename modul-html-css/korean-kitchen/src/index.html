What is korean food?

Korean cuisine is steeped in history, reflecting centuries of cultural exchange and culinary evolution. From its roots in ancient agricultural practices to the influences of neighboring countries like China and Japan, Korean food has continually adapted and innovated over time. Key elements such as fermentation, evidenced in iconic dishes like kimchi, emerged as practical methods of food preservation in Korea's harsh climate. The concept of "banchan" also has deep historical roots, originating from the tradition of serving multiple small dishes to accompany rice, fostering a sense of abundance and hospitality. Today, Korean cuisine stands as a testament to the resilience and creativity of the Korean people, offering a flavorful journey through the country's past and present culinary landscape.
